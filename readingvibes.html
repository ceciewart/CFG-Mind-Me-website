Reading Vibes

Dr. Russ Harris is a famous ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) author, he developed a series of books to clinicians and patients alike. This is pretty much a useful guide to anyone wishing to live a fulfilling life and deal with your own personal struggles.

Surviving a trauma can be often paralising, but books like this will help you to understand better how to move forward and free yourself from bad memories that can mark you so deeply. This book compiles a lot of information from trauma survivors from their stories, neuroscience, techniques, etc!

You don`t know why you feel certain way about a particular topic/ situation? It could be an inherited trauma! This book explores the topic of intergenerational trauma, how it could have been started with your family- not you.

In this book; psychologist, professor and relationship expert Alexandra H. Solomon talks abouts the term relational self awareness. If you are struggling with self-love, and would like to discover your inner being, this is a great read!

A visually beautiful book where you can discover the story of four unlikely friends and learn some of their most important life lessons. This is a great book to open at random pages or read as usual, do what you feel like doing as the author recommends - it's a book to merely experience and reflect about it and find peace and joy in life!

A children's book that every adult should have read! An easy to read story with a powerful message about what we search for in relationships, life itself and what we are truly searching for in our own journeys.

Dr. Brene Brown is a researcher in the field of vulnerability and in this book she brings years of knowledge in groundbreaking research on the topic to every area of someone's life: from difficult conversations, trying something new or meeting new people. A very interesting book to read if you're feeling paralised by fear or anxiety.

As Jenny says: 'You can't experience pain without also experiencing the baffling and ridiculous moments of being fiercely, unapologetically, intensely and (above all) furiously happy.' It's a philosophy that has quite literally saved her life.
